      I was excited to try one of SF's most reputable coffee shops, but unfortunately my visit was very underwhelming... And this is coming from a craft-coffee-loving, cool-space-hunting, hipster-wannabe, Instagramming millennial -_-;; Yes the coffee is good, but not mind-blowingly better than all the many choices for artisan coffee around SF. The menu is simple, with coffee and espresso essentials. But the utopia in this picture-perfect coffee shop shatters once you see the prices, which skyrocket out the (warehouse) roof. I got a mocha, which was a whopping $5.75, AND tax was not included (it was $6.22 total), AND it was served in a tiny cup that looked like a sad 8oz., AND they pretentiously only offer whole milk or housemade almond/macadamia nut milk (extra $1). So if I had been lactose intolerant, I would have to pay over $7 for a small mocha! The mocha itself was quite good, blended with quality Dandelion chocolate for deliciously bitter, dark chocolately notes layered over freshly roasted espresso. It was complex and delectable and all, but overpriced at the end of the day. (Would rather grab some tacos or a bowl of noodles at Yamo in the neighborhood for about the same price) Other observations: - The wait is also quite slow; for all the profit that they make, I only observed one barista making drinks. - Note that there is no wifi, so it's not meant for getting work done. (I dislike how customers' coffee shop uses are sacrificed for better turnover and more profit.) - The ambiance reminds me a lot of coffee shops in Portland, with a roasting section in the back, wooden stools and tables, and exposed beams. The industrial vibe is photogenic, but not really unique in its own right as it looks imported from Portland. - Parking is actually not impossible, at least in the mornings. There are plenty of metered spots, with free parking a short walk away. - If you are going to splurge here, be sure to get your camera phones ready. The latte art is noteworthy and they serve drinks in beautiful stoneware, which I believe they rotate (though when I went, the fancy saucer was a hue of beige that ended up looking like a compostable paper plate in pictures). ***Pro tip: There are a lot of places around the SF Bay Area that serve Four Barrel coffee, at much more reasonable prices (e.g. B. patisserie's Four Barrel mocha is only $4.75, 12oz., tax included!) The verdict? The original Four Barrel location is a neat spot, but too pretentious and overpriced for my tastes.... it costs 2 arms and 2 legs, as if you've been shot by a 4-barrel pistol. Sorry, bad pun xD

        I was excited to try one of SF's most reputable coffee shops, but unfortunately my visit was very underwhelming... And this is coming from a craft-coffee-loving, cool-space-hunting, hipster-wannabe, Instagramming millennial -_-;;

        Yes the coffee is good, but not mind-blowingly better than all the many choices for artisan coffee around SF. The menu is simple, with coffee and espresso essentials.

        But the utopia in this picture-perfect coffee shop shatters once you see the prices, which skyrocket out the (warehouse) roof. I got a mocha, which was a whopping $5.75, AND tax was not included (it was $6.22 total), AND it was served in a tiny cup that looked like a sad 8oz., AND they pretentiously only offer whole milk or housemade almond/macadamia nut milk (extra $1). So if I had been lactose intolerant, I would have to pay over $7 for a small mocha!

        The mocha itself was quite good, blended with quality Dandelion chocolate for deliciously bitter, dark chocolately notes layered over freshly roasted espresso. It was complex and delectable and all, but overpriced at the end of the day. (Would rather grab some tacos or a bowl of noodles at Yamo in the neighborhood for about the same price)

        Other observations:
        - The wait is also quite slow; for all the profit that they make, I only observed one barista making drinks.
        - Note that there is no wifi, so it's not meant for getting work done. (I dislike how customers' coffee shop uses are sacrificed for better turnover and more profit.)
        - The ambiance reminds me a lot of coffee shops in Portland, with a roasting section in the back, wooden stools and tables, and exposed beams. The industrial vibe is photogenic, but not really unique in its own right as it looks imported from Portland.
        - Parking is actually not impossible, at least in the mornings. There are plenty of metered spots, with free parking a short walk away.
        - If you are going to splurge here, be sure to get your camera phones ready. The latte art is noteworthy and they serve drinks in beautiful stoneware, which I believe they rotate (though when I went, the fancy saucer was a hue of beige that ended up looking like a compostable paper plate in pictures).

        ***Pro tip: There are a lot of places around the SF Bay Area that serve Four Barrel coffee, at much more reasonable prices (e.g. B. patisserie's Four Barrel mocha is only $4.75, 12oz., tax included!)

        The verdict? The original Four Barrel location is a neat spot, but too pretentious and overpriced for my tastes.... it costs 2 arms and 2 legs, as if you've been shot by a 4-barrel pistol. Sorry, bad pun xD

        What a funky little place, in a good way. Tucked away off a busy street and next to a tiny hillside park, this Four Barrel has a great little (hipster) hole in the wall vibe.

        If you've visited any Four Barrel before then you already know the coffee is legit but you come to this one for the amazing wood paneling ceiling, the two large garage doors that are rolled up on a nice day, the freshly cut spring flowers on the large wooden tables.

        You don't come here, however, for the pour over coffee (they don't do pour over) or for the restrooms (nope, no restrooms either). Lack of made-for-you-right-then coffee and potty area aside, the espresso drinks are still incredible and the almond butter toasts are to die for.

        If you're a Four Barrel fan, I would recommend stopping by this location for a new experience and setting.
